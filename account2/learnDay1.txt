Unlike the classical 'bits' in our everyday computers that are strictly either a 0 or a 1, a 'qubit' can be both 0 and 1 simultaneously. This incredible phenomenon is called superposition. Think of a coin spinning in the air: it's neither heads nor tails until it lands. A qubit operates in a similar fashion, holding multiple possibilities at once until measured. This unique ability is precisely what gives quantum computers their immense potential for solving complex problems far beyond classical capabilities.
